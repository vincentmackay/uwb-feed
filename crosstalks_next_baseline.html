This page contains crosstalk simulations of a dish not with the closest dish, but with the other dish over it, see figure below.

Here, I compare the crosstalks of the shortest baseline, with those of the next baseline (skip a dish).

The takeaway of this result is that the crosstalks don't drop super fast. If we look back at the simulations where I simply increased the distance between two dishes, presented here, we can roughly expect -1 dB per 20 cm of increased distance. Here, I go from 40 cm to 680 cm, so I whould expect 32 dB less crosstalks, but I only get 13 dB.

Note that this was done with the feed sticking out. I have a different setup where the dish is deeper (f/D=0.23), and the feed is different: it is designed such that it illuminates a deeper dish better, and doesn't need to stick out. Here it is:

Here are the same crosstalks presented above, but for that feed, in the deeper dish:

Here, the takeaway is that if the dish is hidden, the crosstalks drop faster with increased distance: instead of dropping by the order of 10 dB, they drop by nearly 20-25 dB.

Skip-a-dish for 45-degree-rotated setup

I simulated the "skip-a-dish" setup again, this time with dishes that were individually rotated 45 degrees (and with the feed sticking out in the f/D=0.25 dish). We know from my previous simulations that this setup has lower crosstalks at low frequencies, but worse in the middle of the band.

The takeaway here is that again, with the feed sticking out, going to the next baseline only decreases the crosstalks by the order of 10 dB.
