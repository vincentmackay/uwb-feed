I repeated some crosstalk simulations using a dishes with a rolled rim, as presented in the picture below. The radius of the roll is 50 mm, and it is all covered in PEC.

The following plot compare two simulations: one is with the "normal", sharp-rim dish, and one is with the rolled-rim. Note that I fixed the center-to-center distance to be 1 normal dish diameter + 400 mm. The rolled-rim dish has a slightly larger diameter than 6 meters (roughly 6170 mm), so the edge-to-edge distance for that simulation is closer to 230 mm.

Conclusion: the difference is negligible, at least at short distances.

I now tried the same thing, but at 800 mm distance.

Conclusion: the difference is still negligible, by the same amount.
