I simulated the crosstalks within the feed when it is not installed on a dish. These simulations were performed with, and without balun:

  1. Without balun: the port is across the narrow bottom of the slot (this simulation is faster, but not realistic),
  2. With balun: a stripline goes across the narrow bottom of the slot, shorted on one end, and the port is at the other end.

Here are the results:

It is obvious from these simulations that without baluns, the S2,1 cannot be properly simulated: the -170 dB range is effectively zero. However, when simulating the full 2-dish setup, adding the balun will make the simulations significantly longer.

Crosstalks in the full 2-dish system, with and without baluns

I present the dish-to-dish crosstalks here as an argument that the results, when adding a balun, match the ones I would get if I were to simulate the feed alone with baluns, and the full 2-dish system without baluns. My goal is to avoid simulating the full 2-dish system with balun, as it is too computationally expensive.

First, here is a visual representation of the setup of the polarizations and corresponding port numbers:

Let's look at the crosstalk for the most basic setup:

  • 6 m dish,
  • Focal ratio = 0.25 (focus is at the exact same height as the rim),
  • Feed sticks out 218 mm (this is the position of its phase center),
  • Dish distance (rim-to-rim) is 40 cm.
Let's simulate with, and without balun.

Here are the results (note that you can turn curves on and off by clicking on their color in the legend):

Breaking down the results:

  • S2,1
    • Without baluns: -120 dB, which is close to the feed-only S2,1 (-170 dB presented above), plus the expected contribution from the S3,1 (-50 dB).
      • With baluns: Very similar to the feed-only S2,1 with balun.
  • S3,1 and S4,2: the results are the same with or without balun.
  • S4,1: the balun adds >~-20 dB, which makes sense as we've seen that the balun picks up the perpendicular polarization in the S2,1 sims.
Theses results indicate that it makes sense to use feed-only simulations to check for the S2,1's, and no-balun simulations to check for the S3,1's, S4,2's, and S4,1's. This will save a lot of time.
