Some crosstalk simulations

All these simulations were performed with the "large" feed that has a flat ~50% efficiency across the band. This feed sticks out exactly 218 mm (this is the position at which it reaches that good efficiency).

I first simulate that setup in a simple 6 m dish with rim-to-rim distances of 10, 20, 40, and 80 cm. I then simulate the exact same thing, but adding a cylindrical "sleeve" that starts at the edge of the dish and goes up 218 mm (the distance by which the feed sticks out).

These two simulations are presented below. Note that I only present S1,3 (=S3,1) because these are the only S-parameters that are not negligibly small in these simulations (apart from S1,1, S2,2, S3,3, and S4,4). Ports 1 and 3 are each in a different dish, and their polarization plane is that in which the dish translation vector lie, i.e. the ground planes would eventually overlap if we were to extend them (ports 2 and 4 excite the planes that never overlap, that are orthogonal to the dish translation vector). Also, the simulations are performed withouth a balun. Adding the baluns makes the S1,2 (=S2,1=S3,4=S4,3) go up to -35 dB at high frequencies, I will present that further down.
